1. Behaviour Change Toolkit

Objectives

  • Recognise the structure of Very Brief Advice.
  • Use OARS skills from motivational interviewing.
  • Use COM-B to think about barriers to change.
  • Match support to the person's stage of change.

Quick summary

Very Brief Advice: Ask, Advise, Act.

OARS: Open questions, Affirmations, Reflections, Summaries.

COM-B: Capability, Opportunity, Motivation.

Stages of Change: Pre-contemplation, contemplation, preparation, action, maintenance, lapse/relapse.

Written activities

1. Write one example of an open question you could use in practice.

2. Think of a behaviour-change issue you saw on placement. Was the main barrier capability, opportunity, or motivation?

3. Write a short Very Brief Advice sequence for smoking or weight.

2. Smoking Cessation

Key points

  • Offer Very Brief Advice at every relevant contact.
  • Behavioural support plus pharmacotherapy is about three times more effective than going it alone.
  • Combination NRT means a long-acting product plus a short-acting product.
  • Pregnancy and severe mental illness may need specific pathways.
  • Stopping smoking affects clozapine and olanzapine levels.

Checkpoint questions

1. What does the Act step look like in practice?

2. Why does cutting down not reduce harm in direct proportion to cigarette count?

3. What is the medication safety issue when someone on clozapine stops smoking?

Scenario prompt

A client says, “I've cut down from 25 cigarettes a day to 15. That's better, right?”

Write how you would respond in a way that is accurate, non-judgemental and useful.

3. Falls Prevention

Summary topic

High-yield points:

  • Falls are not an inevitable part of ageing.
  • Use a multifactorial assessment.
  • Strength and balance training is one of the strongest evidence-based interventions.
  • Medication review, postural blood pressure, footwear, home hazards and vision all matter.

Practice notes

1. Name three factors you would ask about in a falls assessment.

2. What would make you escalate after a fall?

4. Healthy Weight

Summary topic

High-yield points:

  • Use respectful, neutral, non-stigmatising language.
  • Ask permission before discussing weight.
  • BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis.
  • Consider lower BMI thresholds for some ethnic groups.
  • Be alert to eating disorder features and unintentional weight loss.

Practice notes

1. Write one respectful opener for a conversation about weight.

2. What features would make a routine weight-management approach inappropriate?

5. Mental Health and Wellbeing

Summary topic

High-yield points:

  • Community public health work is often about noticing, asking, responding and signposting.
  • Ask directly about suicidal thoughts if concerned.
  • Do not promise confidentiality you cannot keep.
  • Escalate promptly if there is risk of self-harm, suicide, psychosis, or safeguarding concern.

Practice notes

1. Write one direct but compassionate question you could ask if you were worried about suicide risk.

2. What are the minimum next steps if someone discloses suicidal thoughts?
